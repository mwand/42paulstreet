1    A summer like no other

Congratulations! Youve escaped to the Cape. Were doing our best to make your stay enjoyable. Youll find a clean house (of course!) and fresh pads on the mattresses. Hopefully youve brought sheets, pillows, pillowcases, and towels (including dish towels and pot holders), so youll be ready to go!

Restaurants are starting to open up for outdoor dining, under tents or on patios, and for takeout. Curbside pickup or delivery is available at many groceries and restaurants. Instacart also delivers from several groceries and from CVS. We have not attempted to update the listings below, since things may change. When in doubt, call ahead for details.

Alas, the big venues, like the Cape Playhouse and the Melody Tent, are closed for the season. Well, as we Red Sox fans always used to say, wait for next year.

As you may know, the social-distancing practices in Massachusetts have succeeded in bringing the Covid infection rates down far below their peak, less than many other states. We hope youll practice good social distancing to help keep the curve flattened, and to help keep you (and us!) safe. Most of you have been renting from us for years, so we trust you to have good judgment. Enjoy (especially the sunsets)!

3    The beach

 

The neighborhood beach is at the end of Dr. Lords Road. Be careful going down the slopeits a little steep and it can be slippery.

There is no lifeguard at this beach. If you turn left and walk down 1/3 of a mile, youll come to two town beaches, Howes Street Beach and Corporation Beach, that are supervised by lifeguards. Corporation Beach[2] has picnic tables, rest rooms, and a playground.

Scargo Lake is a spring-fed fresh-water lake just on the other side of Route 6A. It has a nice small beach with a lifeguard and no waves. You can take canoes, kayaks, and small sailboats out on the lake.

Mayflower Beach has won awards for Most Beautiful Beach. It has a long stretch of white sand and extensive sand flats at low tide. It also has a snack bar and rest rooms.

You should drive out to Chapin Beach, where youll find long sand flats, and many kite-boarders on windy days. On the way, enjoy the dunes and the marshits like a little bit of Provincetown right in Dennis. See if you can see any activity on the egret poles on the marsh.

Beach chairs are in the shed. Please brush them off before you put them back in the shed.

 

 

4    food!

4.1  groceries

The nearest grocery is Dennis Public Market (the DPM) on Route 6A in Dennis Village, which has most of the staples you need, along with beer and wine.

Theres a big Stop & Shop in South Dennis on the east side of Route 134 (Exit 9 off Route 6). Across the street, on the west side of Route 134, is Ring Brothers Market. They carry fresh fish from Chatham, a variety of gourmet selections, plus a good-sized wine and liquor department.

For true locavores, visit Not Enough Acres farm (less than a mile away on Sesuit Neck Road; go out Dr. Lords Road and bear left at the fork). Mr. Deck, the owner, has an honor-system farm stand. Be sure to try the honey and the eggs, which are fantastic.

You should also visit CapeAbilities farm, a little ways west of DPM on Route 6A. Fresh, organic produce, plants, and flowers raised by folks with various disabilities. Profits go to disability services.

4.2  Breakfast

Go to the Underground Bakery in Dennis Village (behind the Post Office and down the steps; open 6am to 5pm on weekdays; 6am-330pm on weekends). Ian and Lori have wonderful pastries, sandwiches, and prepared items. Tell them Mitch and Barbara sent you.

If you want a hearty breakfast or lunch, go to Grumpys (on 6A between here and Route 134.) Only open til 2pm. Be prepared to stand on line on the weekend.

Taste-test blueberry muffins at the Other Side Caf (just north of 6A on Route 134) or the blueberry scones at the Sesuit Harbor Caf (on Sesuit Neck Road, hidden behind the Dennis Yacht Club). The muffins at the Other Side are supposedly made with the famous recipe from the long-defunct Jordan Marsh store in Boston. The scones at Sesuit Harbor Caf are also excellent, especially if youre waiting for your fish & chips to come up.

4.3  lunch / informal dinner / fish & chips

There are way too many good places to list, so weve restricted ourselves to places right here on the north side of Dennis.

       Scargo Caf (Dennis Village)

       Chapins Bayside (85 Taunton Avenue, near Mayflower Beach)

       The Marshside (beautiful view of the marsh)

       Sesuit Harbor Caf (watch the boats go in and out of the harbor from the picnic tables; great fish & chips and lobster rolls)

       The Lost Dog (Rte 134 & 6A). Join the locals at this neighborhood pub. Also good pizza.

       Captain Frostys (6A just west of Dennis Village; famous fish & chips, etc., also good soft-serve)

       Kates Seafood (Route 6A in Brewster; also good ice cream).

       Royal Pizza II (Route 6A in Yarmouthport). Greek/Mediterranean food and pizza with a real family feeling.

4.4  ice cream!

Our biggest family disagreements are over which ice cream shop is best. Here are the leading candidates:

       The Ice Cream Smuggler (near the corner of 6A and Route 134.)

       The Sundae School (Lower County Road in Dennisport)

       Kates Seafood (Route 6A in Brewster)

4.5  for a fancier dinner

Bucas Tuscan Roadhouse. On Route 28 in Harwich near the Orleans line. Good Italian food and a great wine list.

Spinnaker, on Route 6A in Brewster, describes itself as a Mediterranean-chic boutique inn and restaurant. A cozy place opened by a nice couple in 2017. Weve eaten there a few times and were impressed with the food.

Brewster Fish House, also on Route 6A in Brewster (where else?). Good fish. Does not take reservations, so be prepared to wait unless you go early.

Possibly the most inventive food on the Cape is to be found at the Ocean House in Dennisport. In past years theyve had a prix fixe special menu in June that is a great bargain. The dining room overlooks the water, so go before sunset, so you can get the full effect. Alas, its kind of noisy, but the food is worth it.

Chillingsworth's on 6A in Brewster is a classic-style French restaurant. They have an umpteen-course prix fixe menu, but you can also order a la carte. They also have a less formal bistro.

Also on 6A in Brewster is the Ocean Edge Resort. The Terrace has elegant outdoor dining.

In Chatham, try the Impudent Oyster (pub atmosphere), or go to the Chatham Bars Inn for a drink at sunset.

5    sightseeing

The Things to do on Cape Cod booklet comes out weekly. You can pick up new copies, with many discount coupons, at the Dennis Public Market and many other locations.

Well mention just a few attractions. You can get more detail on your favorite travel site. Again, call to see whats open and when.

       Scargo Pottery (great pottery and stoneware, right across 6A from us)

       Cape Cod Museum of Art (Dennis Village)

       Cape Cinema (in the same complex as the Cape Cod Museum of Art. An art-house cinema with wonderful murals by Rockwell Kent)

       Cape Playhouse (live theatre; programs change weekly)

       Scargo Tower (great views for the non-acrophobics among us)

       Chatham: picturesque main street with good shopping. Check out Yankee Ingenuity and the Chatham Candy Manor.

       Cape Cod Museum of Natural History (on 6A in Brewster; be sure to go at low tide so you can do the trail walk. Then stop at Kates for ice cream)

       The Lobster Roll: 1 hr. Cape Cod Bay cruises leave from the Sesuit Harbor Caf. Guess what they serve! Several departures daily; go for the sunset tour if you can tear yourself away from the cottage at sunset.

       Cape Cod Rail Trail (paved bike trail; starts in South Yarmouth and goes all the way to Eastham)

       Provincetown: walk the main street and go in the galleries.

       Wellfleet: also good art and shopping; a little closer and way calmer than Provincetown.

       Movies: Besides the Cape Cinema, theres a multiplex in South Dennis with all the latest releases.

       Cape Cod Melody Tent (Hyannis; live concerts)

       The Christmas Tree Shops (several locations): everything you never knew you needed.

       Yeah, if all else fails, theres always the Cape Cod Mall in Hyannis.
